9-1-1 Address Lookup

Check whether a physical address is on record in the Carbon County 9-1-1 database. This database is maintained by the GIS & Addressing Department and is used by emergency services.

Street Address

Enter a street address. Optionally add a community name to narrow results.

About This Database

This tool searches the official Carbon County NG911 site address point (SSAP) database. Not all rural or newly constructed addresses may appear. If your address is missing, use the report button to notify our office. To apply for a new address, use the County Development Permit Package Application.

Property & Parcel Lookup

Search Montana Cadastral records for parcel information in Carbon County. You can search by owner name, geocode (parcel number), or street address.

Search By

Enter a last name or full name. Example: "Smith" or "Smith John"

Data Source

Parcel data is sourced from the Montana State Library Cadastral database and may not reflect the most recent county records. For official parcel information contact the Carbon County Clerk & Recorder.

Floodplain Check

Find out if a property or address falls within a FEMA designated Special Flood Hazard Area (SFHA). This is commonly required for building permits, mortgages, and flood insurance determinations.

Street Address

Include city and state for best results. Example: "456 Broadway Ave, Red Lodge, MT"

Important Notice

This tool provides a general indication based on FEMA's National Flood Hazard Layer. It is not a formal flood zone determination. For official determinations required by lenders or permits, request a FEMA Elevation Certificate or contact our office.

Addressing Authority

The GIS & Addressing Department serves as the addressing authority for Carbon County, maintaining the official database of road names and physical addresses for all structures, primarily in support of 9-1-1 and emergency services.

To apply for a new address, use the County Development Permit Package Application.
